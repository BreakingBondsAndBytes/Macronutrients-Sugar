Macronutrients: Sugar

Sugar is one of three macronutrients and is also known as a carbohydrate

Sugar is a macronutrient and commercial sugar is known as sucrose, a glycosidically-bonded glucose and fructose.
Another term for sugar is carbohydrate. Carbohydrates are composed chemically of carbon and water, the elemental
composition being carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen. The molecular formula for sucrose is C12H24O6. It is important
to realize that the molecular formula for glucose and fructose is the same; however, the structural arrangement is different.
This means that glucose and fructose are isomers. Commercial sugar, or sucrose, contains 4 calories per gram.

The Scientific Method

The Scientific Method is the process through which scientists make discoveries. The six steps are as follows:


  1. Asking a question based on observation
  2. Forming a hypothesis which is an educated guess
  3. Conducting an experiment (three variables):
    • Controlled Variable -- The variables we intentionally keep the same
    • Dependent Variable -- The variable that is responsive to the independent variable
    • Independent Variable -- The variable that is intentionally manipulated
  4. Forming an analysis
  5. Drawing a conclusion
  6. Communicating findings to the audience, scientific or non-scientific

The Scientific Method Applied: Sugar Dissolution Rate with Varying Water Temperatures

A common experiment for journeyman scientists is seeing how fast sugar dissolves when you change the temperature.
Let's explore with simulated results.

Asking a Question Based on Observation

Scenario: You take a medicine pill and dissolve it in water before you drink it every night before bed.
You notice that when you use hot water it dissolves faster; when you use cold water it dissolves slower.
A few weeks later you find out it was a sugar pill being used as a placebo. You begin to wonder if the water
temperature had an effect on the rate at which it dissolved. This drives you to form a hypothesis.

Forming a Hypothesis Which Is an Educated Guess

If sugar is dissolved in water at higher temperatures, then it will dissolve faster

Conducting an Experiment (Three Variables):

Variable Type Definition Instance(s)
Controlled The variables we intentionally keep the same Cups, Water Volume, Sugar Amount
Dependent The variable that is responsive to the independent variable The dissolvation rate
Independent The variable that is intentionally manipulated The water temperature

Important Notes:

Forming an Analysis

Based on the like dissolves like principle it makes sense that sugar would dissolve effectively in water.
With regards to the dissolution rate, we can say that the space in between water molecules increases as the temperature increases.

Conclusion

Our hypothesis was correct, the rate of sugar dissolution in water increases with the increase in water temperature.
